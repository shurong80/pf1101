PF1101 Fundamentals of Project Management

LEARNING OUTCOMES

This module covers the fundamental concepts of project management. It identifies the broad project management knowledge areas with specific reference to applications in the built environment. Students are introduced to the theories and principles relating to project scope, time, cost, risk, quality, human resource, communications, procurement and integration management.

Students are also introduced to the project management processes of initiating, planning, executing, monitoring & controlling and closing. The overall integration of the knowledge areas with project resources and project externalities is also covered. At the end of this module, students will be able to understand different industry practices and applications of project management with examples drawn from both within and outside of the built environment.

The module PF1101 Fundamentals of Project Management consists of 4 Modular Credits (MCs). The MC-value of a module is derived by dividing the estimated total number of workload hours per week for that module by the credit factor of 2.5 (i.e., one MC is equivalent to 2.5 hours of study and preparation per week).

Therefore, this 4-MC module would require 10 hours of work a week, including lectures, tutorials, projects, assignments, and preparatory work.
